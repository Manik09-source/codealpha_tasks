Earthquake-Resistant Building Design Concepts
Mini Project Report | Civil Engineering
Author: Manik Deogam
Overview
This report examines the fundamental principles and modern strategies used in earthquake-resistant building design. It covers seismic force analysis, structural systems such as base isolation, shear walls, and moment-resisting frames, material selection, and code-compliance considerations under IS 1893 and international standards, with worked load estimations and a comparative system analysis.

Key Takeaways
No single structural system is universally optimal — selection depends on seismic zone, building height, occupancy, and budget.
Base isolation is most effective for protecting critical facilities (hospitals, emergency centres) in high seismic zones.
Shear walls offer the best cost-to-performance ratio for buildings up to 35 storeys.
Moment-resisting frames provide architectural flexibility and excellent ductility for mixed-use high-rise structures.
Regular plan geometry, continuous load paths, and ductile detailing are the three pillars of robust seismic design.
